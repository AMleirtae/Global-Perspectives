About this project

Why we're doing this

Global Perspectives challenges us to explore real-world issues through research, reflection, and action. This visit connects theory to life — understanding child welfare, community responsibility, and what it means to serve.

🌍

Global awareness

Connect local realities to global conversations on child welfare, rights, and inequality.

🤝

Community service

Give back through respectful, meaningful engagement with children who need it most.

💡

Critical thinking

Reflect on social systems, inequality, and what real change actually looks like.

❤️

Empathy in action

Build genuine relationships — not a checkbox exercise, but real human connection.

Our plan

How the visit will work

Six weeks, carefully structured to ensure a respectful, joyful, and meaningful experience for the children and our team.

Timeline

1
Step 1–2
Research & preparation
Each member studies child welfare issues and the home's background. We prepare how to engage sensitively and safely.
2
Step 3
Resource collection
Gathering donations — stationery, books, games, hygiene items — in coordination with the home's management.
3
Step 4
The visit day
A full day at the home: structured activities, informal time, and handing over donations. No photos without consent.
4
Step 5-6
Reflection & presentation
Personal reflections, compiled findings, and a school presentation linking the experience to our GP themes.

Visit day activities

🎨

Art & craft session

A creative workshop where children can express themselves freely through drawing, painting, and craft making.

📖

Storytelling circle

Reading aloud and encouraging children to share their own stories in a warm, safe environment.

🏆

Games & play

Team games that build confidence, cooperation, and most importantly — joy.
